Investment Methodology

The Berkeley Investment Group tries to focus its efforts on finding companies that are both expanding sales and profitability in industries with either barriers to entry or steady recurring cash flows. We try to buy these companies at a fair price and try to receive above average returns as companies use their powerful position within the industry to expand their business. As value investors, we do not believe in diversification and believe that every name in our portfolio stands on it own. As a result, we run a concentrated portfolio with 10-15 names where our largest position makes up roughly 12-15% of the portfolio.

Performance

The Berkeley Investment Group generated returns of 31.3% in 2013, 6.6% higher returns than the S&P 500’s 24.7% growth during the same period. Our returns were achieved with a 1.1 beta and a 1.1 Sharpe Ratio. This year was a high-growth year for the Berkeley Investment Group. As a result of 31% returns and an influx of new investors, our fund more than doubled its assets under management. We now manage more than $133,000 in student- investment capital.
